A semi-autonomous mathematical verification council. Several AI models, each in a
fixed adversarial role, are coordinated by Hermes toward one mathematical goal.
The system is built so that agreement between models proves nothing: a claim is
`proven` only when a proof-assistant kernel (Lean) accepts it, `known` only with
a literature citation, and `not_established` by default. Its purpose is not to
flatter progress but to tell the cold truth about what has actually been
established — and to stop a lone researcher from believing a proof that is not
there.
